It’s 3:00 AM. Your shop is closed, your laptop is shut, and the lights are off. But on a server halfway across the world, a bot is knocking on your digital door. It’s not trying to break the lock; it’s trying a key. Then another key. Then another. Thousands of times a minute.

This is Credential Stuffing. It is the silent epidemic of the small business world.

Hackers know you don’t have a million-dollar firewall. They also know that humans are creatures of habit. If you used the password `Mustang1968!` for your LinkedIn account in 2018, and that account was breached, they are betting—statistically, correctly—that you use `Mustang1968!` for your business email, your bank, or your supplier portal today.

They aren't hacking *you*. They are hacking your *habits*.

If you run a small business, you cannot afford to be low-hanging fruit. A single breached email account can lead to invoice fraud that drains your operating capital. But here is the good news: locking this door is free, fast, and entirely within your control.

## The Strategy: 3 Walls of Defense

### Wall 1: Kill the Shared Password (The Manager)
The root cause of credential stuffing is password reuse. If you have 50 accounts and 50 unique passwords, credential stuffing is mathematically impossible against you.
*   **The Tool:** Get a Password Manager. Bitwarden (free/open source) or 1Password.
*   **The Action:** Start today. Don't try to change all 50 at once. Change the "Critical 3" immediately: (1) Your Email, (2) Your Banking, (3) Your Payroll/Accounting. Let the manager generate a 20-character chaotic string for each.

### Wall 2: The Second Key (MFA)
Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) stops 99.9% of automated attacks. Even if they have your password, they don't have your phone.
*   **The Upgrade:** Move away from SMS (text message) codes if you can—they can be intercepted. Use an Authenticator App (Google Authenticator, Microsoft Authenticator) or even better, a hardware key (YubiKey).
*   **The Rule:** If an app offers MFA, turn it on. No exceptions.

### Wall 3: The Early Warning System (HaveIBeenPwned)
How do you know if your "key" has been stolen?
*   **The Resource:** Go to [haveibeenpwned.com](https://haveibeenpwned.com). Enter your business email. It will tell you exactly which data breaches your email has appeared in.
*   **The Habit:** Subscribe to their notification service. If your email appears in a new dump, you’ll know instantly.

## A Note to the Owner
Security isn't just IT. It’s reputation. Protecting your credentials is protecting your client’s trust. Take an hour this week. Install a manager. Turn on MFA. Close the door, so you can sleep soundly at 3:00 AM, knowing the bots are knocking on a wall of stone.
