"Aap gosht nahi khatay? To kya khatay hain? Ghaas?" (You don't eat meat? Then what do you eat? Grass?)
Every Pakistani vegan has heard this joke.
In a country obsessed with BBQ and Nihari, being vegan feels like rebellion.
But here is the secret: **Pakistani cuisine is 70% Vegan by default.**
Daal, Chana, Bhindi, Aloo Gobi—these are all plant-based.
In 2026, the "Plant-Based Meat" revolution has finally hit our grocery stores. Here is how to survive (and thrive) as a Vegan in Pakistan.

---

## 🌱 1. The "Default Vegan" List
You don't need fancy substitutes. These classics are safe.
*   **Daal Chawal:** The national comfort food. (Ask for oil tarka, not ghee).
*   **Chana Masala:** High protein, high flavor.
*   **Aloo Palak:** Spinach and potatoes.
*   **Pakoras:** Chickpea flour fritters. (Totally vegan).
*   **Gol Gappay:** The water is tamarind, the filling is chickpea/potato. Just skip the dahi (yogurt).

---

## 🥩 2. The Meat Substitutes (2026 Update)
*   **Jackfruit (Kathal):** It shreds exactly like beef. Use it for **Vegan Nihari**.
*   **Soya Chunks (Nutrela):** The poor man's meat. Great for Pulao.
*   **Mushrooms:** Oyster mushrooms fried with Tikka Masala taste frighteningly like chicken.

---

## 🥛 3. Dealing with Dairy (The Real Challenge)
Milk is in everything. Chai, Kheer, Naan.
*   **The Chai Hack:** Oat Milk is the best substitute for tea. Soy milk separates. Almond milk is too thin.
*   **The Yogurt Hack:** Use **Coconut Yogurt**. Ideally, make it at home using coconut milk and probiotic capsules.
*   **The Ghee Hack:** Banaspati Ghee is vegetable oil. Desi Ghee is butter. Stick to Banaspati or Olive Oil.

---

## 🍳 4. Recipe Spotlight: Vegan "Chicken" Karahi
**Ingredients:**
*   500g Oyster Mushrooms (torn into strips)
*   4 Tomatoes (Pureed)
*   1 tbsp Ginger Garlic Paste
*   1 tsp Black Pepper (Crushed)
*   Green Chilies (Lots of them)

**Method:**
1.  Fry the mushrooms until golden brown (this creates the "meaty" texture). Remove them.
2.  In the same oil, fry ginger/garlic and tomatoes until the oil separates.
3.  Add the mushrooms back. Add salt and black pepper.
4.  Garnish with ginger julienne and cilantro.
*Result:* It tastes better than 90% of highway karahis.

Being vegan in Pakistan is no longer impossible. It is an adventure.

And the best part? You can still eat 10 Samosas without the cholesterol guilt.
