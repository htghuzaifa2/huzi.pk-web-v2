There is a unique kind of heartbreak when a beautiful piece of technology starts to stutter. Your foldable phone—that marvel of engineering that bends glass and defies physics—is lagging. Or perhaps you are preparing to sell it, ready to upgrade to the latest model, and you need to wipe your digital life from its memory.

Factory resetting a phone used to be simple. But with foldables, the hardware is unique, and the software is often customized to handle dual screens and flex modes. A standard reset is *usually* fine, but to ensure you don't damage the delicate inner screen software calibration or get locked out by theft protection, there is a better way.

This is your Huzi-style guide to wiping the slate clean on your foldable device—be it a Galaxy Z Fold, a Pixel Fold, or a OnePlus Open—safely, securely, and completely.

## Step 1: The Holy Backup (Do Not Skip This)
A factory reset is the nuclear option. There is no "Undo."
*   **Cloud Backup:** Go to Settings > Google > Backup. Ensure "Back up by Google One" is On and tap "Back up now."
*   **Media Check:** Open Google Photos. Look for the "Backup complete" cloud icon. Don't lose those family memories.
*   **The App Authenticator Trap:** If you use 2FA apps (Google Authenticator, Authy), **export your accounts** to a new device or ensure cloud sync is on. If you wipe your phone without this, you might lock yourself out of your bank or email!

## Step 2: The Theft Protection Unlock (FRF)
Modern Android phones have "Factory Reset Protection" (FRP). If you reset a phone without signing out, the next user (or you) *must* enter the original Google password to use it. This is great for theft, bad for selling.

**Crucial Step:** Go to **Settings > Accounts (or Passwords & Accounts)**. Remove your Google Account(s) and Samsung/Manufacturer accounts *before* the reset. This disables the lock and leaves the phone truly "unlocked" for a new user.

## Step 3: The Reset Process
### For Samsung Galaxy Z Fold/Flip Series:
1.  **Unfold the device.** Always perform system operations on the main inner screen for visibility.
2.  Go to **Settings > General Management > Reset**.
3.  Tap **Factory Data Reset**.
4.  Scroll down, review the list of apps/accounts to be wiped, and tap **Reset**.
5.  Enter your PIN/Pattern and tap **Delete All**.

### For Google Pixel Fold:
1.  Go to **Settings > System > Reset options**.
2.  Tap **Erase all data (factory reset)**.
3.  Tap **Erase all data** and confirm with your PIN.

### For OnePlus Open:
1.  Go to **Settings > Additional Settings > Back up and reset**.
2.  Tap **Phone reset > Erase all data**.

## Step 4: The Physical Care (The Foldable Twist)
While the software wipes, treat the hardware right.
*   **Do not fold/unfold repeatedly** during the reboot process. Let it sit flat or folded in a stable position.
*   **Remove the SIM card** tray *after* the reset initiates, while it is rebooting, or before you start.
*   **Clean the Crease:** If you are selling it, now is the time. Use a microfiber cloth. *Never* use alcohol or liquid cleaners directly on the inner screen. A dry, gentle wipe is all it needs.

## A Fresh Start
When the phone boots up to the "Hello" or "Welcome" screen, stop. Do not set it up. Hold the power button and shut it down.
