## 🛠️ Comprehensive Course Breakdown

### 1. DigiSkills.pk (The People's Choice)
If you have zero budget and a lot of passion, this is for you.
*   **Curriculum:** SEO, Social Media, Ads, and Freelancing.
*   **The Catch:** It's self-paced. If you're lazy, you'll never finish it. You need discipline.
*   **Success Story:** One of my friends started here, practiced for 6 months, and now runs a small agency from his home in Multan.

### 2. Google Digital Marketing Certificate (The Resume Booster)
This is what I recommend for serious career-seekers.
*   **Price:** ~$49/month (but you can get it **FREE** via Financial Aid).
*   **Why it's better:** It covers the "Modern" web—E-commerce, Analytics, and Customer Loyalty.
*   **Huzi's Tip:** Apply for Financial Aid on Coursera. Tell them you're a student from Pakistan and can't afford the USD conversion. 90% of the time, they approve it in 15 days.

### 3. MDi & IDT (The Local Powerhouses)
Best for networking. You'll meet local marketing heads and business owners.
*   **Price:** Rs. 40k - 90k.
*   **Benefit:** Hands-on projects. You actually run "Live" campaigns with real (small) budgets.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Can I learn digital marketing without a degree?
Yes. 80% of top digital marketers in Pakistan come from diverse backgrounds (Engineers, Doctors, Arts students). Your skills matter more than your degree.

### How much can I earn as a beginner?
In a local agency, expect **Rs. 35,000 - 50,000**. On Fiverr/Upwork, your first gig might be **$20**, but it can scale to **$500+ per client** per month once you have results.

### Is digital marketing "saturated" in Pakistan?
Generic "Post-kar-do" marketing is saturated. **Data-driven, high-ROI** marketing is NOT. Companies are begging for people who can actually bring them "Sales," not just "Likes."

### Do I need a high-end laptop for this?
No. Any laptop that can run Google Chrome smoothly is enough. Most of your work happens in the browser.
