## 🔥 The "Firewall" Survival Guide: 2025 Edition

In 2025, Pakistan's "National Firewall" is more active than ever. Sometimes, even a premium VPN won't connect on its default settings. Here is how to fight back:

1.  **Switch to Stealth Protocol:** If your VPN has a "Stealth" or "Obscure" mode (like Proton or Nord), turn it on. It makes your VPN traffic look like normal HTTPS web browsing, making it harder for the firewall to block.
2.  **Use WireGuard:** This is the fastest modern protocol. If you're a gamer playing on Singapore or Middle East servers, WireGuard will give you the lowest latency.
3.  **The "Kill Switch" is Mandatory:** In Pakistan, internet drops are common. A Kill Switch ensures that if your VPN drops for a second, your true IP address (and location) isn't leaked to the web.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Are VPNs legal in Pakistan?
As of early 2025, the government requires business and freelance users to "Register" their VPNs with the PTA. However, for personal use, there is no active ban on using reputable VPN services. Always check the latest PTA announcements.

### Does a VPN slow down my internet?
Yes, usually by 5-10%. But in Pakistan, sometimes a VPN can actually *speed up* your connection if your ISP is "throttling" (slowing down) specific sites like YouTube or Netflix.

### Can I use a VPN for online banking?
**Not recommended.** Most Pakistani banking apps (like HBL or SadaPay) will block you if they see you're connecting from a "Foreign IP." Turn off the VPN before opening your bank app to avoid getting your account locked.

### How do I pay for a VPN from Pakistan?
Most major VPNs accept international Visa/Mastercards (SadaPay and NayaPay work great). Some also accept **Crypto (Bitcoin)** if you want extra privacy.
