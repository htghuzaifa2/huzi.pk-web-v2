## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Does "200 Megapixels" actually help?
Only if you want to print a billboard. For Instagram, a 12MP photo is enough. However, 200MP sensors use a technique called **"Pixel Binning"** to combine 16 pixels into 1. This helps a lot in low-light and gives you a much better "Digital Zoom" without the photo becoming a pixelated mess.

### Why do my "In-App" photos (Instagram/Snapchat) look worse?
On Android, most apps take a "Screenshot" of your camera viewfinder rather than using the actual camera sensor. iPhones don't have this problem. **The Fix:** Always take the photo with your phone's default camera app and *then* upload it to Instagram for the best quality.

### Is "Optical Zoom" better than "Digital Zoom"?
**100% Yes.** Optical zoom uses actual moving lenses. Digital zoom is just "Cropping" and enlarging the image, which loses detail. If you like shooting sports or birds, look for a phone with at least "3x or 5x Optical Zoom."

### How do I clean my lens?
Don't use your shirt! Use a **Microfiber cloth** (like the ones for glasses). Pakistani dust contains tiny sand particles that can leave micro-scratches on your camera glass over time, making your photos look "Cloudy."
